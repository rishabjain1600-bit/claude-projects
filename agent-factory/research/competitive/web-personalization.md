# Competitive Research: Web Personalization & Agentic UX
*Tools that adapt websites based on visitor context — the closest adjacent category*

---

### Fibr AI
**Website:** fibr.ai
**Category:** Agentic Web Experience Platform / AI CRO
**Funding:** $7.5M seed (led by Accel + MVP Ventures + WillowTree). Prior $1.8M pre-seed also Accel-led. Total: $9.3M.
**Pricing:** Starter + Enterprise tiers. Unlimited sessions on Enterprise. Custom pricing.

**What they do**
Fibr AI is building the "Agentic Web Experience Layer" — every URL becomes an autonomous, context-aware agent that adapts in real time. Ingests visitor signals (ad interactions, search history, CRM/CDP data, GA4) and rewrites page content, imagery, headlines, and CTAs for each visitor without A/B tests. Runs AI-led micro-experiments continuously. Already deployed by Fortune 50 banks in banking, financial services, and healthcare. Reports 35–50% reduction in acquisition costs for early clients.

**What they do NOT do**
- Personalizes what the visitor sees — does not take actions on the visitor's behalf
- No conversational AI agent that talks to the visitor
- No autonomous browser-side actions (clicking, navigating, form-filling)
- No multi-step plan execution on behalf of the visitor
- No behavioral pattern-to-agent-proposal workflow — adapts content proactively per segment, not per friction event
- Focused on content adaptation, not task completion

**ICP fit**
Enterprise and mid-market brands with high acquisition costs. Early traction in banking and financial services. Buyer is VP Marketing or Head of Growth/CRO.

**Competitive threat to us**
Medium-High — most similar acquisition model (script install + behavioral signals + auto-adaptive) but their bet is content personalization, not agentic action. If Fibr adds a conversational agent and browser action layer, they'd be the closest direct competitor.

**Key quote or signal**
"Websites personalize ads and emails before the click, but the moment someone lands on the website, it still resets to zero." — This is the exact problem we're solving, and Fibr is solving it with content changes. We solve it with agents that act. Same insight, different solution.

### Optimizely
**Website:** optimizely.com
**Category:** Web Experimentation / Digital Experience Platform
**Funding:** Acquired by Episerver (2021), then by San Francisco-based private equity. Private. Revenue ~$300M+.
**Pricing:** Custom enterprise only. Minimum ~$36K/year. Scale tier 40–60% more than Business tier. High-traffic plans can reach $200K+/year. Opal AI features now on credit-based billing (from May 2025).

**What they do**
Optimizely is the legacy leader in A/B testing and web experimentation. Offers feature flagging, personalization, content management, and data platform products. In 2025, launched "Opal" — an AI layer across all products using credit-based usage — and "agent orchestration at scale." Positions toward AI-driven experimentation that reduces the need for manual test setup.

**What they do NOT do**
- A/B testing and personalization require human-designed experiments and variants
- No real-time visitor intervention or agent activation
- No conversational AI agent for visitors
- No autonomous browser-side actions
- Opal AI assists internal teams with experimentation setup — not visitor-facing
- Extremely enterprise-priced — inaccessible to SMB

**ICP fit**
Enterprise digital teams at large consumer and B2B companies. Buyer is VP Product, VP Marketing, or Digital Experience Director. Minimum six-figure annual commitment.

**Competitive threat to us**
Low — legacy experimentation platform pivoting to AI-assisted testing. Not moving toward visitor-facing agents or real-time intervention. Enterprise pricing removes any overlap with our early target market.

**Key quote or signal**
Opal credit-based billing (added May 2025) caused customer frustration — unexpected AI usage costs became a pain point. Pricing complexity at the enterprise level is creating openings for simpler, outcome-based alternatives.

### VWO (Visual Website Optimizer)
**Website:** vwo.com
**Category:** Conversion Rate Optimization / A/B Testing
**Funding:** Bootstrapped. Profitable. Private. Part of Wingify (India-based).
**Pricing:** Starts at $199/month (Testing). Full platform (Testing + Insights + Personalize) custom enterprise. Pricing per monthly tracked users.

**What they do**
VWO is a comprehensive CRO platform: A/B testing, multivariate testing, heatmaps, session recordings, surveys, and personalization. Targets mid-market businesses as a more accessible alternative to Optimizely. AI features include test idea generation and copy suggestions. VWO Personalize delivers rule-based personalization (segment → variant).

**What they do NOT do**
- Experimentation requires human-designed test variants
- Personalization is rule-based — not AI-proposed from behavioral patterns
- No real-time conversational agent for visitors
- No autonomous browser-side actions
- No auto-proposed agents from friction detection

**ICP fit**
Mid-market digital and ecommerce teams. Buyer is CRO specialist, growth marketer, or product manager. More accessible than Optimizely but still requires experimentation expertise to operate.

**Competitive threat to us**
Low — a testing and personalization tool, not an agent deployment platform. Solves conversion problems through experiment iteration (weeks), not real-time intervention (seconds).

**Key quote or signal**
VWO's strength is making CRO accessible to mid-market teams — same customer who might benefit from Agent Factory. If VWO added an AI agent activation layer on top of their behavioral data, they'd have the audience. Not their current roadmap.

### Redscope AI
**Website:** redscope.ai
**Category:** AI Drop-in Agents for B2B/SaaS Conversion
**Funding:** Early stage / bootstrapped. Limited public information.
**Pricing:** Outcome-based — charges per qualified MQL, booked demo, or trial signup. No upfront platform fee disclosed.

**What they do**
Redscope positions as "drop-in agents that read pages, match visitor intent, and trigger the right next step for every visitor, autonomously." Lightweight SDK targeting SaaS and B2B companies — converts evaluators into trials, demos, and signups. Outcome-based pricing model (only pay when the agent produces a result).

**What they do NOT do**
- B2B/SaaS focused — not ecommerce or consumer checkout
- Unclear if it takes multi-step autonomous browser actions or just serves targeted prompts and CTAs
- No evidence of behavioral pattern learning from GA4/GTM to auto-propose new agent types
- No operator dashboard or observer panel showing agent reasoning
- Limited public product information — early stage, opaque

**ICP fit**
SaaS companies wanting to convert more website evaluators. Buyer is growth marketer or head of demand gen.

**Competitive threat to us**
Medium — conceptually the closest startup to Agent Factory's positioning ("drop-in agents, triggered by intent"). But appears to be B2B/lead-gen only and lacks the browser action execution layer and behavioral learning system.

**Key quote or signal**
Outcome-based pricing (pay per MQL/demo/signup) is a smart model that removes risk for early adopters. Worth considering as a pricing approach — aligns with our "the value is execution, not conversation" principle.

## Strategic Implications

**Biggest short-term threat:** Fibr AI — same investor (Accel), same market thesis ("agentic web"), same script install + behavioral signal acquisition model. If they add a conversational layer and browser action execution, they become a direct competitor with a head start on enterprise relationships.

**Most likely to move into our space:** Fibr AI — they're already calling it the "Agentic Web Experience Layer." The gap between content personalization and agentic action is smaller than it looks from the outside.

**Clearest white space we can own:** Action over adaptation. Fibr changes what the visitor sees. We change what happens to the visitor. Nobody else is combining script-install + behavioral learning + conversational agent + browser execution in a merchant-deployable product. That's the gap.
